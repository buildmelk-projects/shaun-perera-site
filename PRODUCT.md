# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Fans discovering Shaun's music for the first time, existing listeners wanting to go deeper, press and media contacts looking for a credible press-ready source, and booking agents or event organisers reaching out directly. They arrive from Instagram, YouTube, or word of mouth. Most are on mobile. Context is casual but the moment matters — first impressions count.

## Product Purpose

The official digital home for Shaun Perera-de Mel, Sri Lankan indie singer-songwriter and voice of Magic Box Mixup. No website existed before this; the site consolidates a scattered online presence into one authoritative source. Success means a visitor walks away knowing exactly who Shaun is, hearing his music, and knowing how to reach him.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, Intimate, Polished.

The site should feel like a well-shot music video still frame — dark, considered, a little bit dramatic — but never cold. Shaun's music is about human connection; the design should hold that warmth underneath the cinematic surface.

## Anti-references

Generic music-template sites (Squarespace musician defaults, Bandcamp vanilla pages). Anything that feels mass-produced or interchangeable with any other artist. Neon-on-black EDM aesthetics. Overcrowded tour-date-heavy layouts. Anything that shouts rather than draws you in.

## Design Principles

1. **Draw in, don't shout.** Restraint is the move. Let negative space and typography do the heavy lifting before any decorative element does.
2. **Warmth inside the dark.** The palette is dark and cinematic but never cold — the gold accent and text warmth carry the intimacy.
3. **Every section earns its scroll.** No filler. If a section doesn't tell you something new about Shaun, it shouldn't exist.
4. **The music is the product.** Design frames the music; it never competes. Streaming links, YouTube embeds, and song titles are always the sharpest elements on screen.
5. **Sri Lankan without being a cliché.** Shaun's roots matter but the site isn't a tourist brochure. Cultural identity comes through in the warmth of the palette and the weight of the language, not in symbols or flags.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG target stated. Aim for AA contrast on all body text. Respect prefers-reduced-motion for entrance animations. The bilingual Sinhala text (Unicode) must render correctly across all platforms.
